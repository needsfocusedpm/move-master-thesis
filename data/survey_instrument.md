# Survey Instrument – Needs-Focused Leadership

## Overview

This survey examines the impact of needs-focused leadership across project and functional environments.

It is structured into four parts:

1. Role identification  
2. Perception-based Likert-scale questions  
3. Leadership competency ranking  
4. Demographic and contextual data  

---

## Part 1: Role Identification

### Q1 – Primary Role in Relation to Projects

Please select the option that best describes your current role:

- Project Manager – I lead and manage projects.  
- Project Team Member (Expert) – I contribute specialized knowledge or skills to projects.  
- Project Sponsor / Executive Decision Maker – I own projects from the business side and make high-level decisions.  
- Other / None – I’m not currently involved in projects.  

---

### Q2 – Functional Role (if not project-related)

- Functional Team Lead – I directly lead and manage a team within my functional area.  
- Functional Expert – I contribute specialized knowledge or expertise, but do not lead people directly. 
- Functional Executive Leader – I manage multiple teams or a whole functional area and make executive-level decisions. 

---

## Part 2: Perception of Needs-Focused Leadership

### Scale

All items were measured on a 5-point Likert scale:

1 – Strongly disagree  
2 – Disagree  
3 – Neutral  
4 – Agree  
5 – Strongly agree  

---

### Q3 – Project Managers

1. I regularly engage with my team members to understand their individual professional and personal needs.  
2. My efforts to recognize individual team members’ needs visibly enhance their motivation and job satisfaction.  
3. I have observed that my team’s performance improves when I explicitly address individual needs. 
4. I actively discuss career aspirations and personal goals with team members to encourage their retention. 
5. Applying a needs-focused approach has noticeably reduced turnover in my project teams.  
6. I am convinced that addressing team members’ personal and professional needs reduces the likelihood of team members leaving.  
7. Actively considering the individual needs of my team members positively impacts the achievement of project objectives (time, budget, scope/quality).  
8. I have noticed fewer major conflicts or delays in projects where I employ a needs-focused leadership style.  

---

### Q4 – Project Team Members (Experts)

1. My project manager actively seeks to understand and accommodate my personal and professional needs. 
2. My motivation to perform well is directly influenced by how effectively my individual needs are recognized by my project manager. 
3. I notice our team performs better when our project manager explicitly addresses our individual needs. 
4. I am more likely to stay on a project or within the organization if my individual needs are consistently understood and addressed by the project manager.  
5. Recognition and consideration of my individual professional and personal needs significantly influence my decision to remain in the project team. 
6. Feeling genuinely supported by my project manager regarding my personal and career needs makes me less inclined to seek other opportunities.
7. I believe our team achieves better project results (timeliness, quality, efficiency) when the project manager clearly addresses team members’ individual needs.
8. Projects led by managers who actively engage with our individual needs experience fewer conflicts or misunderstandings that could negatively impact project outcomes.

---

### Q5 – Project Sponsors / Executives

1. Project managers who focus explicitly on individual team members’ needs create noticeably more motivated project teams.
2. I have observed increased job satisfaction among teams led by project managers who actively manage team members’ personal and professional needs.  
3. I consider needs-focused leadership an essential factor for fostering high-performing project teams. 
4. I observe lower team member turnover rates in projects managed with a clear focus on individual team members’ needs. 
5. Consistent recognition of team members’ individual needs by the project manager positively influences team members’ desire to remain on the project or in the organization.  
6. From my executive viewpoint, project managers who actively address individual needs experience significantly fewer team member departures.  
7. Projects managed with explicit attention to team members’ personal and professional needs are more likely to meet deadlines, budgets, and quality standards.  
8. I observe that projects led by needs-focused project managers tend to experience fewer significant conflicts or delays.

## Part 3: Leadership Competency Ranking

### Q9 – Ranking Task

Participants ranked the following competencies (1 = most critical, 9 = least critical):

- Emotional Balance: Resilience, self-awareness, calm under stress  
- Assertiveness: Respectfully expressing personal and team needs  
- Identifying and Managing Personal Triggers: Self-reflection, mindfulness to stay neutral 
- Attentive and Empathetic Listening: Active, respectful understanding of concerns 
- Service-Oriented Leadership: Prioritizing team members’ individual needs  
- Facilitating Open Dialogue and Feedback: Two-way communication and transparency  
- Impartial Thinking: Objectivity and fairness in decisions  
- Systems-Oriented Problem Solving: Seeing connections and root causes  
- Creating Clear Paths to Action: Turning needs into structured steps  

---

### Q10 – Open Reflection

Participants were asked to explain their ranking and suggest additional competencies.

(Note: Free-text responses were excluded from the structured dataset.)
